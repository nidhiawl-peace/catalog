Purpose of This Catalog

This catalog provides a structured reference of every class of organisational document that exists in a professionally governed enterprise — across 25 functional domains spanning governance, operations, clinical practice, craft production, hospitality, and purpose-led work.

It serves three functions: Inventory — a complete map so nothing is unnamed or overlooked. Decision Support — enabling rapid identification of what is missing or available to commission. Communication — a reference for leadership to assess documentation maturity and prioritise next initiatives.

The gap between what exists in an organisation and what is listed here is where risk, inefficiency, and institutional knowledge loss reside.

How to Read This Catalog

Each entry represents one distinct document type, organised within 25 functional domains. Four columns per entry:

# Reference number within the domain. Type The named document deliverable. Purpose What the document achieves and why it exists, in plain language. Driver The compliance framework, standard, or governance obligation that requires or informs it. Not a prerequisite — a ceiling of what the document can achieve when fully developed.

LP Maturity Framework — Every Engagement Calibrated to Your Stage

L1 Foundation First policies and procedures. Ad hoc processes begin to be documented.
L2 Working System Core documents exist. Processes are followed but not yet centrally governed.
L3 Governed Practice Defined, documented, approved, and consistently followed. Audit-ready.
L4 Measured and Improving KPIs, reviews, and corrective systems drive continuous improvement.
L5 Self-Renewing Ecosystem Knowledge flows, adapts, and evolves with the organisation autonomously.

When a brief does not specify the level needed, Lucent Prism assesses current documentation maturity through a structured intake process before any work begins. You do not need to know what you need — that is LP’s job to find out. The quality standard applied remains constant across all levels. The depth, complexity, and expression of the document changes.
