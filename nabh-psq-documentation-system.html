The Difference Between a Document and a System

Quality indicator sheet — updated quarterly. Safety committee minutes — filed since 2021. On paper: compliant. In practice: the forms are filed and forgotten, the indicators tracked but never analysed, the committee minutes recorded but the actions never verified.

PSQ Core Elements — What Assessors Check Without Exception

Three elements in PSQ are CORE — assessed mandatorily at every visit: PSQ.1.a (patient safety programme implemented by multi-disciplinary committee), PSQ.3 (quality indicators monitored and used for improvement), PSQ.7.a (incident management system implemented). Core elements must score 4 or above. A score of 2 or below on any single standard blocks accreditation regardless of overall performance.

Classification Governance Document — Tier 2 Distribution All Clinical and Non-Clinical Staff Supersedes v1.0 (January 2023)
1. Purpose

To establish a systematic, non-punitive process for reporting, investigating, analysing, and acting on incidents across all departments — ensuring patient safety and continual quality improvement in alignment with NABH 6th Edition Standard PSQ.7. This policy creates a culture where incidents are reported because action follows, not despite fear that it will not.

2. Scope

All clinical and non-clinical staff across all departments, all shifts, all patient care areas and support services. Applies to incidents involving patients, visitors, and staff.

3. Incident Classification
CategoryDefinitionExamples
Near Miss An incident that could have caused harm but did not reach the patient due to chance or timely intervention Medication prepared in error but not administered; mislabelled sample caught before processing; wrong patient identified before procedure
Adverse Event An unintended harm to a patient arising from healthcare management rather than the underlying clinical condition Patient fall resulting in injury; wrong medication administered; healthcare-associated infection; pressure ulcer development
Sentinel Event An unexpected occurrence involving death or serious physical or psychological injury, or significant risk thereof Wrong-site surgery; retained surgical instrument; patient suicide on premises; infant abduction; transfusion reaction resulting in death
4. Reporting Protocol and Escalation Matrix
CategoryReport ToTimeframeForm Reference
Near Miss Department Head Within 24 hours [Hospital Code]-PSQ-FOR-007
Adverse Event Department Head + Quality Manager Within 4 hours; verbal report immediate [Hospital Code]-PSQ-FOR-007
Sentinel Event Medical Director + Chief Executive Verbal: immediate. Written: within 1 hour [Hospital Code]-PSQ-FOR-007 + [Hospital Code]-PSQ-FOR-008

Note: Immediate patient care takes priority over documentation in all cases without exception.

5. Analysis Requirements
  • Near Miss: Trend analysis conducted monthly by Quality Manager. Department Head informed of patterns within 5 working days of monthly review.
  • Adverse Event: Department-level Root Cause Analysis completed within 72 hours. Report submitted to Quality Manager on Form [Hospital Code]-PSQ-FOR-009.
  • Sentinel Event: Full Root Cause Analysis completed within 7 days. Presented to Patient Safety Committee. Corrective Action Plan approved by Medical Director within 14 days.
  • Trend Review: All incidents reviewed at monthly Patient Safety Committee meeting. Quarterly summary report to Medical Director and Board.
6. Non-Punitive Reporting Commitment

This hospital operates a non-punitive incident reporting culture. Staff who report incidents in good faith are protected from punitive action. The purpose of incident reporting is learning and system improvement, not individual blame. Department heads are accountable for creating an environment where reporting is safe and expected.

7. Review and Governance

This policy is reviewed annually by the Quality Manager and approved by the Medical Director. The review considers: incidents from the preceding year, changes to NABH standards, and recommendations from the Patient Safety Committee. Document Owner is accountable for ensuring the review occurs and the revised version is distributed.

The Ownership Column Is What Makes This a System

Every document kit provides a quality indicator template. The column that commodity kits do not build is the ownership column — because without it, the register is a reporting tool, not an accountability tool. LP assigns named owners, sets thresholds with mandatory action triggers, and builds the monthly review cycle into the department head's calendar. When the NABH assessor asks who is responsible for Hand Hygiene Compliance — the answer is in the register, not in someone's head.
