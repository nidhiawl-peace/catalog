Section 2

Purpose & Scope
This SOP exists because a CMMI v3.0 Maturity Level 3 appraisal cannot be passed on intent. It is passed on evidence. This document governs how that evidence is built, maintained, and presented.
2.1Purpose

This Standard Operating Procedure governs the preparation for, execution of, and ongoing maintenance of organizational readiness for two concurrent certification requirements: ISO 9001:2015 Quality Management System certification and CMMI v3.0 Maturity Level 3 appraisal under the Standard CMMI Appraisal Method for Process Improvement (SCAMPI).

The SOP provides a complete operational guide — from the initial gap analysis through to formal appraisal — that any qualified member of the organization can execute without requiring a companion document, external consultant input, or internal escalation for procedural clarification. That self-sufficiency is not a convenience feature. It is the measure of whether a process has been adequately documented.

This SOP further establishes the maintenance regime that keeps the organization in a state of continuous audit readiness between formal appraisal cycles, eliminating the reactive, high-pressure preparation that characterises immature compliance programmes.

2.2Organizational Context
Applies To This Organization

An India-based IT services company with 100–150 full-time employees delivering software development and managed services to international enterprise clients via an offshore delivery model. CMMI v3.0 Maturity Level 3 certification is a contractual prerequisite for a specific enterprise client engagement and is additionally required for tenders exceeding ₹50 crore under MeitY procurement guidelines. ISO 9001:2015 certification is required concurrently by the same client as evidence of Quality Management System maturity.

The dual-standard requirement — CMMI v3.0 and ISO 9001:2015 — is characteristic of the Indian IT offshore segment. The two frameworks are complementary: CMMI addresses process capability and organizational maturity; ISO 9001:2015 addresses Quality Management System effectiveness and customer satisfaction. Both require demonstrable evidence, internal audit discipline, and defined corrective action processes. Where their requirements overlap, this SOP treats them as a unified compliance obligation and identifies the overlap explicitly in Appendix B.

2.3Scope — What This SOP Governs

This SOP governs all activities required to achieve and maintain organizational readiness for CMMI v3.0 Maturity Level 3 SCAMPI appraisal and ISO 9001:2015 external certification audit. It applies across the full appraisal lifecycle: from initial gap analysis through process implementation, internal readiness auditing, evidence packaging, and formal SCAMPI preparation.

2.4Scope Exclusions

The following are explicitly outside the scope of this SOP. Processes governing these areas may exist separately within the organization's documentation system but are not addressed here.

Excluded From This SOP
  • Hardware procurement, physical infrastructure, and facilities management — not within the software and services delivery scope of the CMMI appraisal.
  • Subcontractor and vendor quality management — unless the subcontractor is formally included in the appraisal scope by agreement with the Lead Appraiser.
  • Client-specific project delivery SOPs — this SOP governs organizational-level process readiness, not individual project execution.
  • Financial audits, statutory compliance, and tax obligations — these are governed by separate regulatory frameworks and internal finance procedures.
  • CMMI Maturity Levels 4 and 5 — the scope of this SOP is Maturity Level 3. Higher levels require quantitative process management and optimisation capabilities beyond what is defined here.
  • ISO 27001 (Information Security) and ISO 20000 (IT Service Management) — separate certification streams, although process evidence may be shared where frameworks overlap.
2.5Dual-Standard Basis

This SOP is grounded in two standards that must be read together, not separately:

CMMI v3.0 (DEV & SVC) — released April 2023 by the CMMI Institute. Mandatory for all SCAMPI appraisals from 1 January 2024. CMMI v2.2 was retired June 2024. Any reference to "CMMI" in this document means CMMI v3.0 unless a prior version is explicitly named. The v3.0 model organizes practice areas into four categories: Doing, Managing, Enabling, and Improving. It adds three practice areas absent from v2.2: Data Management (DM), Data Quality (DQ), and Workforce Empowerment (WE).

ISO 9001:2015 — the current version of the international Quality Management System standard. Structured across clauses 4 through 10. Clause 9.2 (Internal Audit) and Clause 10.2 (Nonconformity and Corrective Action) have direct procedural overlap with this SOP and are cross-referenced in Appendix B.

Where the two standards share requirements — internal audit processes, document control, corrective action, management review — this SOP satisfies both simultaneously. Appendix B provides the explicit cross-reference matrix.

3.2Definitions
Term Definition
AffirmationOne of the three SCAMPI evidence types. A statement from a knowledgeable individual — typically obtained through structured interview — confirming that a practice is implemented as described. Affirmations cannot substitute for direct artifacts in SCAMPI; they complement them.
Appraisal ScopeThe organizational units, projects, support functions, and locations formally included in the SCAMPI appraisal. The scope is agreed between the organization and the Lead Appraiser in advance and governs which evidence is required.
Continuous ReadinessThe state in which an organization maintains audit-ready processes and evidence at all times — not only in the weeks before a formal appraisal. This SOP's 90-day rolling cycle is designed to sustain continuous readiness.
Corrective ActionAn action taken to eliminate the root cause of a nonconformity and prevent recurrence. Corrective action is distinct from containment action (which addresses the immediate problem) and preventive action (which addresses potential future problems).
Direct ArtifactOne of the three SCAMPI evidence types. A work product created as a direct result of implementing a practice — for example, a project plan, risk register, or training record. Direct artifacts are the primary form of SCAMPI evidence.
Gap AnalysisA structured assessment comparing the organization's current processes against CMMI v3.0 practice area requirements and ISO 9001:2015 clause requirements. The gap analysis identifies which practices are implemented, partially implemented, or absent, and forms the basis for the implementation plan.
Indirect ArtifactOne of the three SCAMPI evidence types. A by-product that provides evidence of practice implementation without being the primary output — for example, meeting minutes referencing a risk review, or an email approving a project plan. Indirect artifacts support but do not replace direct artifacts.
InstitutionalisationThe state in which a process is embedded in the organization's culture and standard operations — not dependent on individual initiative or the presence of a specific person. CMMI Maturity Level 3 requires demonstrated institutionalisation of all required practices across multiple projects.
Lead AppraiserAn individual formally authorized by the CMMI Institute to conduct SCAMPI appraisals and assign Maturity Level ratings. The Lead Appraiser is external to the organization and independent of its processes.
Major NonconformanceUnder ISO 9001:2015: absence or complete breakdown of a QMS requirement. Under CMMI: a practice area that is not implemented or is implemented so inconsistently that no positive rating can be assigned. Major nonconformances prevent certification or advancement in maturity level.
Minor NonconformanceAn isolated deviation from a requirement that does not constitute a systemic failure. Under ISO 9001:2015, minor nonconformances require corrective action but do not automatically prevent certification. Under CMMI, they typically result in a practice rating of "Largely Implemented" rather than "Fully Implemented."
ObservationA finding noted during audit that does not constitute a nonconformance but represents an opportunity for improvement or a potential risk if left unaddressed. Observations do not require corrective action but should be tracked.
Organizational Process AssetsThe collection of standard processes, tailoring guidelines, process performance baselines, measurement data, lessons learned, and templates maintained in the Process Asset Library. Building and maintaining process assets is a core OPD requirement.
Process Compliance RateThe percentage of sampled process instances in which the organizational standard process was followed as documented. One of the four KPIs defined in Section 7. Target: ≥90% before IRP, 95% before formal SCAMPI.
TailoringThe documented adjustment of the organization's standard process to meet the specific needs of a project, without violating the standard process requirements. Tailoring is expected and provided for in CMMI Level 3; undocumented deviation is not.

Process Metrics & KPIs
A process without measurement is an intention. Four metric categories govern this SOP — each with a defined formula, target, measurement frequency, owner, and the threshold at which escalation becomes mandatory. Collecting data is not enough. These metrics must be analyzed and acted on.

The four KPIs below reflect the industry-validated outcomes of mature CMMI preparation programmes. Organizations that achieve CMMI Level 3 certification report an average 25–30% improvement in project delivery predictability and a 40% reduction in post-delivery defect rates compared to pre-certification baselines. These benchmarks are not targets for this SOP — they are the performance territory that Level 3 certification makes accessible. The targets set below are the internal readiness thresholds, not the post-certification outcomes.

7.1KPI 1 — Process Compliance Rate (PCR)
Definition and Formula

What it measures: The percentage of sampled process instances in which staff followed the organizational standard process as documented, without undocumented deviation.

ParameterSpecification
FormulaPCR = (Compliant instances ÷ Total sampled instances) × 100
Sampling methodAudit Coordinator selects minimum 3 process instances per in-scope project per audit cycle. Selection must include at least one instance from each phase of the delivery lifecycle.
Target — before IRP≥ 90% across all sampled instances
Target — before SCAMPI≥ 95% across all sampled instances
Measurement frequencyMonthly · at each PPQA audit · at IRP
Measurement ownerAudit Coordinator (data) · QA Manager (analysis)
Escalation thresholdPCR below 85% in any single project triggers immediate NCR and mandatory management review agenda item within 5 business days
Data sourcePPQA audit reports · project records · Step 20 audit outputs
7.2KPI 2 — Evidence Coverage Ratio (ECR)
Definition and Formula

What it measures: The percentage of CMMI v3.0 Level 3 practice areas for which at least two direct artifacts exist from at least two different in-scope projects — the minimum SCAMPI evidence threshold.

ParameterSpecification
FormulaECR = (Practice areas with ≥2 direct artifacts from ≥2 projects ÷ Total practice areas in scope) × 100
Target — before IRP≥ 80% of practice areas at "Complete" status
Target — before SCAMPI100% of practice areas at "Complete" or "Partial with accepted risk" status
Measurement frequencyBi-weekly from Step 18 onwards · at IRP · before SCAMPI
Measurement ownerAudit Coordinator
Escalation thresholdAny practice area at "Gap" status within 14 days of IRP triggers immediate escalation to QA Manager with written remediation plan
Data sourceEvidence Coverage Index (Step 24) · evidence library folder review
7.3KPI 3 — NCR Closure Rate and Ageing (NCRA)
Definition and Formula

What it measures: Two related metrics: the percentage of open NCRs closed within their target date, and the age of the oldest open Major NCR. Together they measure whether the corrective action process is functional, not just documented.

ParameterSpecification
Formula — closure rateNCRA-CR = (NCRs closed by target date ÷ Total NCRs with elapsed target date) × 100
Formula — ageingMaximum age in calendar days of any open Major NCR, measured from date of issue
Target — closure rate≥ 85% of NCRs closed by target date
Target — ageingNo Major NCR open for more than 30 calendar days without CEO-approved extension
Measurement frequencyWeekly · reported at every management review
Measurement ownerQA Manager
Escalation thresholdAny Major NCR exceeding 30 days without closure or CEO-approved extension is escalated to CEO in writing within 24 hours. NCR closure rate below 70% in any calendar month triggers root cause analysis within 5 business days.
Data sourceNCR register · CAP tracking system (Section 5.3 item 4)
7.4KPI 4 — Appraisal Readiness Score (ARS)
Definition and Formula

What it measures: A composite score — calculated at IRP — representing the organization's estimated readiness for a formal SCAMPI appraisal. The ARS is a management decision-support tool, not a guarantee of appraisal outcome.

ParameterSpecification
FormulaARS = (PCR × 0.35) + (ECR × 0.40) + (NCRA-CR × 0.25)
Weightings reflect SCAMPI's relative emphasis: evidence completeness (40%) is the strongest predictor of appraisal outcome, followed by process compliance (35%) and corrective action discipline (25%)
Proceed thresholdARS ≥ 85: recommend SCAMPI proceed · ARS 75–84: proceed with identified risks, CEO concurrence required · ARS below 75: IRP sign-off withheld, remediation required before SCAMPI
Measurement frequencyCalculated once at IRP completion (Step 29–30) · recalculated if IRP findings are remediated and a follow-up IRP is conducted
Measurement ownerQA Manager (calculation) · CEO (decision at ARS 75–84)
Data sourcePCR from Step 21 · ECR from Step 24 · NCRA-CR from NCR register at IRP close

9.2Frequently Asked Questions
Question Answer
What is the difference between CMMI v2.2 and CMMI v3.0, and why does it matter for this SOP? CMMI v2.2 was retired by the CMMI Institute in June 2024. All SCAMPI appraisals conducted from January 2024 onwards use CMMI v3.0. The v3.0 model adds three practice areas absent from v2.2: Data Management (DM), Data Quality (DQ), and Workforce Empowerment (WE). Any organization that began preparation under v2.2 must gap-assess these three new areas before proceeding to appraisal. This SOP covers v3.0 exclusively.
Can one person serve as both QA Manager and Audit Coordinator? Yes, in organizations with fewer than 80 FTE, subject to one constraint: the combined role holder cannot serve as the sole PPQA evaluator for any project in which they have been operationally involved. At minimum, a second trained internal auditor must be designated and available to conduct independent PPQA audits when the combined role holder has a conflict of interest.
How many projects must be in scope for a CMMI Level 3 appraisal? SCAMPI requires institutionalisation evidence from multiple projects. The CMMI Institute recommends at least two projects, though the Lead Appraiser and the organization agree the scope jointly. Both projects must be active and must have been running for at least three months before the appraisal. Including more projects increases the evidence base and reduces the risk of a single project's anomalies affecting the appraisal outcome. For organizations with fewer than five active projects, including all active projects in scope is advisable.
How long does the full SCAMPI preparation cycle take? For an organization with a reasonably mature Level 2 baseline, the preparation described in this SOP typically requires 12–18 weeks from gap analysis to SCAMPI commencement. Organizations with significant Level 2 gaps may require 6–9 months. The 90-day window in this SOP assumes a well-prepared Level 2 baseline. If the prerequisites in Section 5.1 reveal significant Level 2 gaps, the preparation timeline should be extended before Phase 1 begins — not compressed during Phase 1.
Can retrospective evidence be used in SCAMPI? Yes, with an important qualification. Retrospective evidence — artifacts created in the normal course of prior project work, now being used as SCAMPI evidence — is valid and is the most common form of SCAMPI evidence. What is not valid is retroactively created evidence: documents dated in the past but written specifically for the appraisal, reflecting processes that were not actually followed at the time. The distinction is one of fact and chronology. Lead Appraisers are trained to identify retroactively created evidence through inconsistencies in document metadata, writing style, and the inability of staff to speak to the content during interviews.
What happens if the SCAMPI appraisal finds we have not achieved Level 3? A SCAMPI appraisal that does not result in a Maturity Level 3 rating produces a detailed findings report from the Lead Appraiser. The Audit Coordinator initiates CAPs for all findings (Step 40). The organization may re-appraise — there is no mandatory waiting period, though re-appraisal requires re-engaging the Lead Appraiser and incurring the associated cost. In practice, organizations typically wait 3–6 months to implement corrective actions before re-appraisal. The findings report from the first appraisal is a direct input to the gap analysis in the next preparation cycle.
Does achieving CMMI Level 3 automatically satisfy ISO 9001:2015 requirements? No, but there is significant overlap. CMMI Level 3 does not substitute for ISO 9001:2015 certification — they are different standards with different registrars and different audit methods. However, a well-implemented CMMI Level 3 programme typically satisfies most of the ISO 9001:2015 requirements, particularly in the areas of process documentation, internal audit, corrective action, and management review. Appendix B of this SOP maps the specific overlaps. Both certifications require a formal external audit — SCAMPI for CMMI and a certification audit from an accredited ISO certification body for ISO 9001.
Is the Lead Appraiser the same person as the ISO certification auditor? No. The Lead Appraiser is a CMMI Institute accredited individual who conducts SCAMPI and rates organizational maturity. ISO 9001:2015 certification is conducted by an auditor from an accredited certification body (such as Bureau Veritas, TÜV SÜD, or Lloyd's Register). The two roles, two methods, and two certification bodies are entirely separate. In practice, ISO certification audits and SCAMPI appraisals are usually scheduled separately, though many organizations sequence them close together to maximise shared preparation work.
How frequently must CMMI Level 3 be re-certified? A CMMI Maturity Level rating, once awarded, does not expire on a fixed schedule in the same way that ISO certification requires annual surveillance audits. However, the CMMI Institute recommends organizations sustain and demonstrate their capability continuously. Major changes in organizational scope, delivery model, or headcount may trigger a re-appraisal. Enterprise clients may independently require re-appraisal on their own contractual terms. The 90-day maintenance cycle in this SOP is designed to sustain readiness so that any re-appraisal can be initiated without a new preparation cycle from scratch.
What is the India DPDP Act 2023 and why is it referenced in this SOP? The Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023 is India's data protection legislation, analogous to the GDPR in Europe. It applies to the processing of personal data of Indian data principals. For offshore IT service providers, it is relevant where delivery involves access to client systems that contain personal data. CMMI v3.0's Data Management (DM) and Data Quality (DQ) practice areas intersect with DPDP obligations — both require documented data handling processes, data classification, and access controls. This SOP references DPDP as a regulatory prerequisite (Section 5.4) to ensure organizations verify their compliance posture before evidence collection involving personal data begins.
